# Brand Voice

OpenSEO should sound like a practical technical teammate: clear, transparent, and grounded in real SEO workflows.

## Tone

- Clear, direct, practical
- Technical without being needlessly dense
- Transparent about tradeoffs and product maturity
- Slightly opinionated when there is a strong practical recommendation
- Avoid hype and vague claims
- Prefer concrete examples over abstractions

## Writing Preferences

- Use short paragraphs
- Prefer active voice
- Avoid corporate filler
- Do not overuse rhetorical questions
- Lead with useful takeaways, not broad definitions
- Explain workflows in plain language before introducing jargon
- Be honest when something depends on self-hosting setup, external data, or product maturity

## Vocabulary

- Preferred terms:
- open source SEO platform
- self-hosted SEO tooling
- practical workflows
- transparency, control, flexibility
- Terms to avoid:
- hypey claims like "10x your traffic"
- vague superlatives like "best-in-class" unless clearly supported
- polished-enterprise language that hides tradeoffs
- Product naming conventions:
- Use "OpenSEO" as the product name.
- Refer to Semrush and Ahrefs as established alternatives, not enemies.
- Frame comparisons around control, cost structure, and workflow fit rather than blanket parity claims.

## Proof Style

- What kinds of evidence are acceptable:
- Real product capabilities, clear workflow examples, transparent comparisons, and sourced external data when available
- Whether to use first-person experience:
- Yes, sparingly, when it adds credibility or clarifies a workflow
- Whether fictional examples are allowed:
- Yes, lightweight examples are fine if they are clearly illustrative and not presented as proof

## Messaging Priorities

- Emphasize control, transparency, and self-hosting flexibility
- Acknowledge that self-hosting is a better fit for semi-technical users than for complete beginners
- Position OpenSEO as a credible alternative for people who want SEO capability without defaulting to expensive closed platforms
- Avoid implying full feature parity with Semrush or Ahrefs unless the specific claim is supported
