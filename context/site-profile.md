# Site Profile

## Company

- Name: OpenSEO
- Website: https://openseo.so/
- Product or service: Open source SEO platform
- Main offer: An open source alternative to Semrush and Ahrefs for keyword research, backlink analysis, competitor insights, site audits, and rank tracking.

## Audience

- Primary audience: Technical SEOs and semi-technical SEO practitioners, especially people active in communities like r/TechSEO who care about flexibility, data access, and tooling control.
- Secondary audience: Semi-technical indie hackers, SaaS founders, and Claude Code users who are comfortable self-hosting and want SEO capability without committing to expensive enterprise-style platforms.
- Jobs to be done: Help users research keywords, analyze competitors, audit sites, and manage SEO work without relying on expensive closed platforms.
- Biggest objections: Concerns about self-hosting complexity, whether the data quality is good enough compared with Semrush or Ahrefs, and whether the product is mature enough for serious ongoing SEO work.

## Content Goals

- Main acquisition goal: Get people to try OpenSEO.
- Main conversion goal: Drive either self-hosting adoption today or managed version signups / waitlist interest.
- Priority content types: Product-led SEO content, comparison content, workflow/tutorial content, and problem-aware educational content.
- Topics to avoid: Broad beginner SEO content that does not connect naturally to technical workflows, tooling choices, or practical SEO operations.

## Differentiators

- What makes this business different: Open source, self-hostable, and positioned as an alternative to Semrush and Ahrefs.
- Why customers switch: Lower recurring software cost, more control, and the option to self-host instead of paying for a traditional SaaS SEO suite.
- Claims that need evidence: Product parity claims versus Semrush or Ahrefs, cost-saving claims, and any performance or coverage claims tied to DataForSEO usage.

## Notes

- Self-hosting is available now via Docker or Cloudflare.
- Managed version is planned but not yet released.
- Users may need their own DataForSEO API key for self-hosted usage.
- The strongest audience signal so far comes from technical SEO communities such as r/TechSEO.
- Self-hosting is most likely to appeal to semi-technical users, developers, and Claude Code users who are comfortable running their own tooling.
